
# Risk Management

## Definitions

| Terminology                           | Definition                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| ------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Benefit                               | Positive impact or desirable outcome of the use of a medical device on the health of an individual, or a positive impact on patient management or public health. Benefits can include positive impact on clinical outcome, quality of life, diagnostic outcomes, or public health.                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| Fault Tree Analysis (FTA)             | A failure analysis in which an undesired state of a system is analyzed using Boolean logic to combine a series of lower-level events.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| Harm                                  | Injury or damage to the health of people, or damage to the property or environment.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| Hazard                                | A potential source of harm.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| Hazard Analysis (HA)                  | A systemic process used to assess risk. Hazard analysis is a top-down analytical method that examines condition, event, or circumstance that could lead to or contribute to an unplanned or undesirable event.  The examination includes the evaluation of the likelihood of the circumstance and it leading to harm, as well as the nature of the harm.                                                                                                                                             |
| Hazardous Situation                   | Circumstances in which people, property or the environment are exposed to one or more hazards.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| P1                                    | The first component of the probability of occurrence value for FMEAs representing the probability of occurrence of the hazardous situation.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| P2                                    | The second component of the probability of occurrence value for FMEAs representing the probability of the hazardous situation leading to harm.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| Probability of Occurrence of Harm (P) | The combination of the probability of a hazardous situation occurring (P1) and the probability of that hazardous situation resulting in the identified harm (P2). P = P1*P2 for FMEAs                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| Reasonably Foreseeable Misuse         | use of a product or system in a way not intended by the manufacturer, but which can result from readily predictable human behavior.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| Residual Risk                         | risk remaining after risk control measures have been implemented.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| Risk                                  | The combination of the probability of occurrence of harm and the severity of that harm                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| Risk Analysis                         | systematic use of available information to identify hazards and to estimate the risk.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| Risk Assessment                       | overall process comprising a risk analysis and a risk evaluation.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| Risk Control                          | Process in which decisions are made and measures implemented by which risks are reduced to, or maintained within, specified levels.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| Risk Estimation                       | process used to assign values to the probability of occurrence of harm and the severity of that harm.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| Risk Evaluation                       | process of comparing the estimated risk against given risk criteria to determine the acceptability of the risk.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| Risk Management                       | The systematic application of management policies, procedures, and practices to the task of analyzing, evaluating, controlling, and monitoring risk.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| Risk Mitigation                       | The efforts taken to reduce either the occurrence or severity of consequences of a hazard; also known as Risk Control Measure.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| Severity                              | Measure of the possible consequences of a hazard.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| Software of Unknown Provenance (SOUP) | SOFTWARE ITEM that is already developed and generally available and that has not been developed for the purpose of being incorporated into the MEDICAL DEVICE (also known as “off-the- shelf software”) or software previously developed for which adequate records of the development PROCESSES are not available.                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| State of the Art                      | developed stage of technical capability at a given time as regards products, processes and services, based on the relevant consolidated findings of science, technology and experience. The state of the art embodies what is currently and generally accepted as good practice in technology and medicine. It does not necessarily imply the most technologically advanced solution. The state of the art described here is sometimes referred to as the “generally acknowledged state of the art”. |
| Verification of Effectiveness (VoE)   | testing used to ensure that the risk control measure (including information for safety) as implemented actually reduces the risk. Verification, Validation or Summative Usability are types of testing that may be used for VoE.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| Verification of Implementation        | Verification testing that confirms by objective evidence the implementation of a risk control measure.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| Use Error                             | user action or lack of user action while using the medical device that leads to a different result than that intended by the manufacturer or expected by the user.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |

### Description of Rating Systems
#### Severity
- Measures the consequences of a hazardous situation aft it has occurred
- Severity can only be changed by product design, not manufacturing controls
- Severity of harm to patient or end user
- Severity is based entirely on the harm, not the probability

#### Probability
- Probability of Occurrence of Harm (P)
	- Probability is dependent on P1 and P2
	- These aren't exactly actual probabilities form events actually occurring but rather just probability estimates
	- Probability rating is used with severity to determine individual and residual risk

| Scale | Qualitative Term | Quantitative Definition                                                                                |
| ----- | ---------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1     | Remote           | Less than 1 in 100,000 procedures (≤ 0.00001) (≤10-5)                                                | 
| 2     | Low              | More than 1 in 100,000 but less than 1 in 10,000 procedures (>0.00001 and ≤0.0001) (>10-5 and ≤10-4) |
| 3     | Moderate         | More than 1 in 10,000 but less than 1 in 1000 procedures (>0.0001 and ≤0.001) (>10-4 and ≤10-3)        |
| 4     | High             | More than 1 in 1000 but less than 1 in 100 procedures (>0.001 and ≤0.01) (>10-3 and ≤10-2)             |
| 5     | Very High        | More than 1 in 100 (>0.01) (>10-2)                                                                     |

- Probabilities aren't actually based in quantitative data
- Procedures are defined by treatment sessions. Dependent on device ussage

| Relative Value | P1 Occurrences          | P2 % of occurrences that harm |
| -------------- | ----------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| 1              | Every procedure         | 100% of occurrences           | 
| 2              | Once a day              | 75% of occurrences            |
| 3              | Once a week             | 50% of occurrences            |
| 4              | Once a month            | 25% of occurrences            |
| 5              | Once a year             | 10% of occurrences            |
| 6              | Twice in life of device | 1% of occurrences             |
| 7              | Once in life of device  | Less than 1% of occurrences   |

- P1 and P2 are assumed to be independent
- P1 is the probability a hazardous situation occurs
- P2 is the probability of harm given a hazardous situation has occurred
